# Home Town

Home Town is a life simulation game. The player is in control of a small town with approx 200 residents. The residents go about their daily lives, meeting their basic needs, socializing, going to school and work, and otherwise acting in the world in accordance with their nature.

Agents are divided into family units (which can be a single agent) and own / rent a lot which is their home. They are able to spend their money on changing the lot, by carrying out building work and purchasing items to decorate with and/or use. They can join various groups, attend schools, visit recreational areas such as parks and cinemas, play in sports teams, and go on holiday. As the game progresses I plan to add ever more richness as to what agents can do in the game world.

The game is 2D with top-down graphics and uses a pop-up modal system with 2D pixel art to help illustrate key moments and add more life to the game.

# USP

The game moves through time. By default the game starts with a 1950s feel. The items available, the kinds of items and styles on offer. And then the time moves forward. Every six in-game months the player gets a new catalogue - pixel art full screen asset that shows them items that they can order for their home. These catalogues help illustrate the flow of time. The design of the covers and interior change, and the items come and go. Early catalogues include vintage styles, and advertise new fangled items like hair dryers and kitchen whisks. By the time the player is in 1990 the catalogue is now a desktop computer website and offers things such as games consoles, and portable casset players. By 2020 the catalogue is a smart phone screen, and sells high tech goods and modern offerings.

This is combined with a yearly news paper / web page that shows some key headlines to help show the passage of time too.
