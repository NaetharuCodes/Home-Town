The game will have ~200 agents that live in a small town.

Agents are divided up into family units. Each family unit holds between 1 and 10 agents (most have 2 to five).

Family units own lots in the world. These are defined rectangles of tiles that are theirs to build on and do with as they please. This is the same concept as we see in a game like The Sims 3. They can move home (purchase a new lot and sell their old one). And agents that grow up to adulthood can also move out, as can some portion of the family when it breaks up for various reasons.

The MPV has lots that are always just single family units. Further down the line I am interested in adding in apartment blocks with shared dwellings that that is not an issue for now.

There are also public lots. These contain public areas that include office buildings, factories, parks, police stations and other areas of a similar kind that the characters have access to beyond their own dwelling.

Agents have relationships with each other. They have blood relations (family) and they also have a oppinion of others. This is broken down into sub-values for a number of metrics such as attraction, respect, and shared interests.

Agents have a number of stats that represent their basic needs:

- hunger
- thirst
- toilet
- sleep
- fun
- achievement
- companionship

Agents also have a number of metrics that represent their skills, abilities and traits. These are simple keyword/value pairs. This is based off the Crusader Kings idea of traids that allows us to have a dumb data driven system and then allow the game to simply read these and respond with the game logic where needed. For example, an athletic agent may swim faster, where as a criminal agent may have different options to steal or mug someone.

Agents can have jobs, and go to school

Agents start as babies, and go through life stages

- newborn
- baby
- toddler
- small child
- big child
- teen
- young adult
- adult
- elder
- venerable
- ancient

Agents have a heath metric which shows how likely they are to die from health related issues. This is very low in default cases until they reach elder. At which point the risk goes up fast, and the chances of death are high at the upper-elder / venerable stage. Very few agents ever make it to ancient!

Agents can modify and build their homes. They can hire an architect to add new walls / floors. They can order new items from a catalogue, which are then delivered (in the post for small items, and via a truck for big ones like a new fridge or bed).

Agents can decorate their homes. They can change the colors of their walls, carpets and furnature etc. There is a lot of freedom in this decoration.

Agents can dress in different outfits.

Agents can talk to each other and carry out daily actions. Chatting with friends, watching TV, cooking and eating food, visiting the park, going to the store, heading to work, going to school, and many other things.

Actions off the home base (work/school etc) can be treated as rabbit holes to start with and then detailed observable and interactive versions can be added as modular updates down the road - a module that fleshes out the police officer career, or one that adds in a football game that they can actually play etc.

Agents age up as time passes (the speed of aging is user-adjustable - we can set it to around the same as The Sims to default).

In the final game there will be seaons an weather. From heatwaves in the summer to snowfall in the winter. Each season will have different chances for weather events.

In the final game there will be festivals and gatherings. Birthday parties, school discos, xmas holidays, and more.
