# Agents are the main characters in the game.

Agents come in a few flavors

1: The player controls a specific family. These are the "player agents" and consist of the family members that live together on a specific game lot. The player is able to give direct control instructions to them to override their normal behaviors. In the game options we will offer the choice of keeping autonomoy on or turning it totally off for the selected family member. Off means they do nothing other than what the player instructs. On means they continue to meet their needs and obligations, but the player controls all other interactions.

Non-selected family members are run with the automated actions.

2: Other families in the world - these are not player controlled and go about their business based on the automated scripting. They have the same level of detail and needs as the player. We may need to look to lower the fidelity of their simulations based on performance (for example, we could do some simplified stats calcs in place of specific needs) but let us test performance before we go down that route.

3: Thin agents are characters that are not fleshed out and who have no needs. They are spawned to do a specific task then removed from the game. The postman, the builder, and other characters that are merely there to flesh out the vibe of the town for a short one off interaction.

For full agents (types 1 and 2) the scripting should be in a priority order

1: Core needs such as sleep, food, drink, and toilet are handled first. If these get below a critical level the agent seeks to address them urgently at the expense of other actions. An agent will pee before going to work, even if that means being late. They will sleep before going to socialize even if that means missing a party.

2: Obligations such as work and school come next. These are actions that the agent will seek to complete as high priority. In effect actions that if missed or failed could have serious consiquences (loss of job, bad grades, missing a key sports match).

3: Minor actions then come next. Things like surfing the web, going for a job, playing cards, watching TV and the like. These are things that agents love to do and it often fills up their fun and social meters. But they are less time critical than the above. They come after them.

There are certain things that might sometimes change this. For example, an agent could be an addict and have a specific need pushed to the top - in extreme cases above even critical based needs. But these are edge cases.

Agents should seek to address their needs in a slightly flexible way - for critical needs they should look to address them between 50% and 25% - if the need falls below 25% then it becomes urgent and an agent will seek that above all else. This gives the game a bit more life, so that they do not act like robots always keeping the exact same rhythm.
